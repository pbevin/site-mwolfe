Diagnosis

When Menya went to the breast clinic on April 17th, it was just one of life's little inconveniences. Her family doctor had quite correctly told us that given her age and medical history, it was 95% likely to be a cyst. I didn't even bother going in with her. 
From: Pete Bevin
Date: Fri, 19 Apr 1996 11:10:25
Subject: Bad news about Menya

Guys,
On Wednesday, Menya was told that she has breast cancer.
The diagnosis isn't complete yet, although we do definitely know that
it's cancer.  They're putting her through various tests in the next
couple of weeks to find out whether it's spread to anywhere else,
and based on that, they'll recommend a course of treatment, which will
probably involve chemotherapy and surgery.
I have to tell you that this is pretty serious.  The lump is pretty
big, and Menya is in the age group where it tends to be more acute.
Even when they treat this, there's a chance of recurrence.
We still don't know what we're going to do.  We're trying to decide
whether it would be best for Menya to be treated here or back home
in Toronto -- it's a pretty difficult decision.  We'll know more when
we know what the treatment is going to be.
Obviously, we'll keep you all up to date.  Please don't tell people who
don't already know.  I know that's hard, but it may be important to us
to have people around who don't know.  (Partners are fine...)  And
please keep in touch, especially if we leave the country.  Your support
through all this will be very valuable.
Pete and Menya.

I got a phone call at 11:30am from the hospital, asking me to come at once. All they would tell me over the phone was that she had been given "bad news". As I drove the 20 miles to the hospital, I tried to figure out how serious it might be. Was it the hospital being over-dramatic, was it that Menya had a week to live - or was it somewhere in between?

It wasn't a hard decision to move to Canada. We threw a party to say goodbye to our friends, closed down our affairs in Britain as best we could, and bought two plane tickets to Toronto.
