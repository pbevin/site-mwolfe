Medical History - Menya Wolfe

April 1996 Diagnosed with inflammatory breast cancer, nodes positive.
May-October 1996 High dose EC with neupogen.
November 1996 MRM showed complete pathological response.
January-February 1997 Radiation to chest wall and nodes.
September 1997 Diagnosed with solitary 3.5cm metastasis to left parietal lobe, clear workup.
October 1997 Craniotomy (complete resection), tumour ER-.
November 1997 Whole brain radiation (5 x 4Gy).
September 1998 Diagnosed with 2cm local recurrence of brain met, clear workup. Stereotactic radiosurgery (15Gy at 95% line).
December 1998 Began thalidomide 400mg/day, escalating doses.
January 1999 After initial reduction, regrowth to almost 2cm. Craniotomy (complete resection).
March 1999 MRI showed narrow margin of enhancement lining surgical cavity. Stopped thalidomide after reaching 700mg/day.
May 1999 MRI showed increased enhancement and symptoms worsened.
June 1999 Began high-dose Methotrexate w/ Leucovorin rescue, 4 doses bi-weekly at 1, 3, 5, and 5 g/m2. MRI showed increased enhancement.
July 1999 MRI showed increased enhancement. Cavity almost completely filled and enhanced area measures about 3cm diameter. Stopped Methotrexate.
August 1999 Started high dose EC with neupogen (as in 1996).
Sept 1999 MRI showed slight increase, so EC dropped because of potential toxicity.
October 1999 Started Temodal on 28-day cycle.
December 1999 No tumour growth on MRI and slight improvement in symptoms. MRS showed no viable tumour in necrotic mass. Stopped chemo on advice of doctors, who reinterpreted all the 1999 MRIs as SRS-related necrosis.
February 2000 Some increase in symptoms, but MRI essentially unchanged.
April 2000 Symptoms much worse, but attributed to hormones or seizure activity.
May 2000 Deficits increasing rapidly, and MRI eventually done a month early. Tumour had doubled or tripled since February, and edema was extensive. Third craniotomy with PDT, again a complete resection, resulting in more right-sided weakness. Post-surgical CT clear.
July 2000 CT shows local regrowth to 1.3 cm with considerable edema. Temodal restarted on 5/28 day cycle.

Menya is currently 35 years old, and there is no evidence of disease outside the brain. There has never been any metastasis anywhere else, and apart from the cancer, she is otherwise healthy. The tumour is in the left parietal lobe, and is affecting her ability to walk (she has a noticeable limp), and to perform fine motor tasks with her right hand. She also reports being less articulate than normal (although she is very articulate to start with) and suffers from occasional focal seizures in her right arm or leg.
